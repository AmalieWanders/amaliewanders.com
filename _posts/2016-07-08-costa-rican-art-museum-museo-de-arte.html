Costa Rican Art Museum (Museo de Arte Costarricense, La Sabana Park), formerly the main building of San Jose’s first international airport, contains a large and impressive collection of Costa Rican visual artworks dating back to colonial times. 

Most of the art pieces on display are sculptures and paintings. I was surprised that one of the exhibits consisted only of the sculptures of the artist Juan Manuel Sanchez Barrantes’s muse Berta. I guess it’s love. 

For the first time in my life, I had a chance to see murals - large format paintings carrying social or political messages, an important characteristic of art in las Americas. 

The best thing - the museum is for free.
